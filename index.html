1. Carpet Kit Layout — CCS0361

The standard TR6 carpet set (CCS0361) comprises 10 numbered pieces. Always fit in numerical order — sills first, front floor mats last. The front mats (#10) overlay the front floor and are the final layer.

2. Tools & Materials

Adhesives

ProductUseNotes
Evostik / Weldwood contact cement (brush-on)Sills — permanent bondApply both surfaces, allow to go touch-dry (~10 min), then press together. No repositioning.
3M 90 / Tetrosyl aerosol spray adhesiveLight anchor — all other piecesApply sparingly to both surfaces. Allows slight repositioning. Do NOT saturate.
Moss Motors #409-036 aerosolGeneral carpet fitApply to both surfaces, allow both to go tacky, press firmly, use rubber mallet.
Warning: Do not over-glue. A light mist is all that is needed for non-sill pieces. Too much glue makes carpets stiff, lumpy, and impossible to remove later.

Fixings (Floor Mat Clip System)

The TR6 uses a specific metal clip system for the floor mats — no glue on the main floor pieces:

  1. A spiky pronged stud is push-fitted through the carpet from below.
  2. A silver retaining clip locks over the prong on top.
  3. A matching receiver stud is pop-riveted to the floor pan.
  4. Carpet clips onto stud — held firmly but removable.
Tip: If original studs are missing, replacement pop-rivet studs are available. Use a 1/8" drill bit for stud holes through the floor mat carpet (per Moss guide).

Full Tool List

  • Sharp trimming knife or carpet shears
  • Steel ruler / long straight edge
  • Tape measure & chalk / pencil
  • Rubber mallet or wooden block (pressing seams)
  • Heat gun or hair dryer (stretching around curves)
  • Isopropyl alcohol (surface degreasing)
  • Masking tape (temporary hold while glue sets)
  • Pop rivet gun (floor stud fitting if needed)
  • 1/8" drill bit
  • Small stiff brush (applying brush-on cement)
  • Old newspaper / cardboard (templates)

3. Preparation

Strip the Interior

  1. Remove seats, sill plates, and any trim retaining clips.
  2. Pull out all old carpet, felt, and underlay.
  3. Remove loose or flaking sound deadening (if replacing — optional but recommended).
Important: Label and bag all clips, screws, and fixings as you remove them. TR6 fixings are hard to source.

Floor Pan Inspection

  • Inspect for rust, especially footwells and under the rear seats.
  • Any rust must be treated (Hammerite, POR-15, or similar) before carpeting.
  • Check pop-rivet studs for floor mats are present and secure. Replace any missing ones now.
  • Ensure there are no sharp edges or protruding metal — these will wear through carpet.

Sound Deadening (Optional but Recommended)

Many TR6 restorers add sound deadening before fitting carpet. The TR6 is a noisy car and deadening makes a significant difference to the cabin. Butyl-based peel-and-stick mats (e.g. Dynamat, Kilmat, STP Gold) work well on the floor pan and transmission tunnel. Apply to clean, dry metal.

Tip: Even a partial application to the footwells and tunnel reduces road/transmission noise noticeably.

Surface Preparation for Gluing

  • All surfaces to be glued must be clean, dry, and grease-free.
  • Wipe down with isopropyl alcohol (IPA) and allow to dry fully.
  • Do this for both the car panel and the back of the carpet piece.

Felt / Bypass

The original TR6 used bitumen felt underlay beneath the carpets. When refitting with new carpet:

  • Use the old felt as a cutting pattern for new pieces.
  • Cut coin-sized holes through the felt to allow the carpet clip studs to pass through.
  • Do not glue felt to the underside of the carpet. Keep them as separate layers.

4. Dummy Fit (Always Do This First)

Before applying any adhesive, dummy fit every single piece. This is the most important step — it costs nothing and prevents expensive mistakes.

Why Dummy Fit?

  • Confirms each piece reaches its edges without pulling or bunching.
  • Identifies cutouts needed (steering column, gear lever, handbrake, pedals).
  • Allows you to mark overlaps and underlaps before committing.
  • Reveals any pieces that need trimming or adjustment.

Dummy Fit Process

  1. Work in numerical order — #1 sills through to #10 front mats.
  2. Lay each piece in position without glue. Use masking tape to hold temporarily.
  3. Check all edges: no raw edge should be visible from inside the car.
  4. Mark cutout positions (steering column, gear lever, handbrake, pedal box) with chalk on the back of the carpet.
  5. Note any pieces that need trimming — mark but do not cut yet.
  6. Check all adjacent pieces overlap or underlap correctly — no gaps, no raw edges showing.
  7. Once all pieces are dummy fitted and checked: remove them in reverse order (#10 first, #1 last), keeping them in sequence ready to glue.
Jonny's Rule: Always dummy fit first. Every time. Even experienced fitters skip this and regret it.

5. Underlay & Felt

What the TR6 Uses

The original TR6 used bitumen-impregnated jute felt as underlay. This provides cushioning, sound absorption, and insulation. New kits typically include replacement felt.

Using Original Felt as a Template

The original felt pieces are excellent cutting patterns for new underlay:

  1. Carefully remove old felt and keep it in one piece where possible.
  2. Lay old felt onto new jute/felt material and trace around it.
  3. Cut new pieces to match.
  4. Cut coin-sized holes through felt at stud positions (floor mats) so clips can pass through cleanly.
Do not glue felt to the underside of the carpet. Keep felt as a loose layer on the floor pan. Gluing them together makes future removal very difficult and can cause the carpet to deform.

Underlay for Non-Original Fitments

Some restorers use modern alternatives:

  • Jute hessian — closest to original, good acoustic properties
  • Foam-backed carpet — no separate underlay needed if carpet has foam backing
  • "Poolboy" style closed-cell foam — popular on TR Forums for better insulation
Tip: If using foam-backed carpet (many John Skinner kits have this), a separate felt underlay layer is not required under the main floor pieces. The backing does the same job.

Felt on Vertical Panels

Vertical panels (inner wings #3, bulkhead #2, tunnel sides) do not use felt underlay. Carpet is glued directly to the panel surface. Sound deadening (if fitted) sits between the metal panel and the carpet.

6. Carpet Fitting — Piece by Piece

Follow the numerical order strictly. Read each piece's notes before gluing.

General Rule: Apply adhesive sparingly to both surfaces. Allow to go touch-tacky before pressing together. Press firmly with a rubber mallet or wooden block — working from the centre outward.

Piece #1 — Sills (L & R)

1 Clean sill face thoroughly with IPA.

2 Apply contact cement (brush-on) to both sill face and carpet back. This is the one place you use full-strength contact cement — it is a permanent bond.

3 Allow both surfaces to go touch-dry (~10 minutes).

4 Carefully position carpet on sill — you have one chance. Press firmly. Mallet along full length.

5 Fold bottom edge under sill lip neatly. Tuck top edge under sill plate.

Sills are permanent. Take extra care on positioning during dummy fit.

Piece #2 — Front Bulkhead

The trickiest piece. The front bulkhead (firewall/toe board) has complex curves and the steering column passes through it.

1 Cut the steering column hole — use the dummy-fit marks. Start small and enlarge carefully.

2 Option: Some experienced fitters cut this piece in half horizontally (upper and lower). Each half is easier to manoeuvre into position. The join is hidden behind the instrument binnacle or by the front mat edge.

3 Apply light spray adhesive to both surfaces. Press upper half first, then lower.

4 Ensure bottom edge tucks behind / is overlapped by front floor mat (#10).

Pieces #3 — Front Inner Wings (L & R)

These carpet the vertical inner face of each front wing/fender.

1 Apply light spray adhesive to both surfaces.

2 Press to panel face. Use heat gun gently on any curved sections to help the carpet conform.

3 Trim to fit arch shape if needed. No raw edges visible from cabin.

Piece #4 — Diff Cover

Covers the differential hump at the rear.

1 Apply light spray to both surfaces.

2 Press over hump, working from centre outward to sides.

3 Rear edge overlaps rear floor mats (#9). Ensure seam is hidden.

Pieces #5, #6, #8 — Tunnel Carpets

Three pieces cover the transmission tunnel from front to rear:

  • #5 Front Tunnel — gear lever area forward. Mark and cut any switch/fastener holes before fitting.
  • #6 Mid Tunnel — gear lever slot. This is a precise cut — mark, cut small, test fit, enlarge gradually.
  • #8 Rear Tunnel — handbrake slot. Same approach for handbrake cutout.

Tunnel pieces butt-join tightly — no overlaps, no gaps. The gearbox gaiter carpet tucks under #5 and is held by the weight of the piece above it.

Apply light spray adhesive to both surfaces. Press from centre outward. Use masking tape to hold edges while adhesive cures.

Piece #7 — Parcel Shelf

The rear parcel shelf / deck behind the seats.

1 Apply light spray only — this piece needs to be removable for hood access.

2 Press flat. Ensure it sits flush with no bubbles.

Pieces #9 — Rear Floor Mats (L & R)

Rear footwell mats. Use the metal clip system — no glue.

1 Position mat over floor stud locations.

2 Push spiky pronged stud through carpet from below at each stud point.

3 Fit silver retaining clip over prong on top surface.

4 Press mat onto pop-riveted floor studs. Should snap firmly into place.

5 Felt underlay sits below mat — coin-sized holes cut at stud positions allow clean clip-through.

Pieces #10 — Front Floor Mats (L & R) — FIT LAST

These are the final pieces — they overlay the front floor and cover the lower edges of the bulkhead and inner wings.

1 Same clip system as rear mats (#9).

2 Position mat, ensuring it overlaps lower edge of bulkhead (#2) and inner wings (#3).

3 Clip firmly onto floor studs.

4 Check all edges: no raw carpet visible anywhere.

7. Finishing & Final Checks

Final Inspection Checklist

  • ✅ No raw carpet edges visible from inside the cabin
  • ✅ All clip-in mats (#9, #10) secure and not lifting at edges
  • ✅ Sills (#1) sitting flush — no lifting at edges
  • ✅ Tunnel pieces (#5, #6, #8) butt-joined neatly — no gaps
  • ✅ Gear lever slot (#6) cut cleanly and gearbox gaiter seated correctly
  • ✅ Handbrake slot (#8) cut cleanly — handbrake moves freely
  • ✅ Steering column cutout (#2) clean — no carpet snagging on column
  • ✅ Parcel shelf (#7) flat with no bubbles
  • ✅ Diff cover (#4) smooth over hump
  • ✅ All glued pieces pressed firmly — use mallet along all seams

Re-fitting Trim & Seats

  1. Refit sill plates / kick panels — these should now sit on top of sill carpet edges, hiding them.
  2. Refit seats. Bolt down firmly — seat runners sit on top of floor mats.
  3. Refit gearbox gaiter surround (sits over #6 mid tunnel carpet).
  4. Refit handbrake surround (sits over #8 rear tunnel carpet).

Curing Time

Contact cement (sills) reaches full strength after 24 hours. Avoid heavy use during this period. Spray adhesive on lighter pieces is typically tack-firm within a few hours.

Tip: Vacuum the whole interior once complete — loose fibres from new carpet are normal and settle after a few weeks of use.

8. Common Issues & Solutions

ProblemLikely CauseSolution
Carpet lifting at sill edgeInsufficient contact cement / contaminated surfacePeel back, clean with IPA, re-glue with brush-on contact cement. Allow both surfaces to dry fully before pressing.
Bubbles under glued carpetToo much adhesive / trapped airWork slowly from one edge outward. Use rubber mallet to press firmly as you go. Prick large bubbles with a pin and press flat.
Floor mat won't clip securelyMissing / corroded pop-rivet stud on floor panDrill out old rivet, fit new stud with pop rivet gun using 1/8" bit.
Gear lever slot too tightCut too small during fittingRemove mid tunnel (#6), enlarge cut carefully. Easier to cut more off than add back.
Raw carpet edge visibleWrong overlap sequenceCheck fitting order. In most cases the adjacent piece should tuck under to hide the raw edge.
Carpet won't lie flat on bulkhead curvesTight compound curvesUse a heat gun on low setting to warm carpet, then press to curve while warm. Hold until cool.
Carpet stiff / lumpy after gluingToo much adhesive usedPrevention: always use sparingly. Rectification: difficult — heavy solvent application may soften, but replacement is often easier.
Front floor mat catching on pedalsMat not clipped / shifting forwardEnsure all clips are engaged. Check stud positions match mat holes exactly.
Note: The front bulkhead (#2) is the piece most commonly fitted incorrectly. If in doubt, cutting it in half (upper and lower) makes it significantly easier to handle.
